  • The noun is pronounced /'delɪgət/. The verb is pronounced /'delɪgeɪt/. 名词读作/'delɪgət/。动词读作/'delɪgeɪt/。

  • 1.N-COUNT代表;(尤指)会议代表A delegate is a person who is chosen to vote or make decisions on behalf of a group of other people, especially at a conference or a meeting.

  • 2.VERB授(权);把(职责、责任等)委托(给)If you delegate duties, responsibilities, or power to someone, you give them those duties, those responsibilities, or that power so that they can act on your behalf.语法信息
    • 【语法信息】: V n to n
    • 【语法信息】: V n to n
    • 【语法信息】: V
    • 【语法信息】: Also V n

    He plans to delegate more authority to his deputies...

    他打算把更多的权力交给副手。

    How many of their activities can be safely and effectively delegated to less trained staff?...

    他们的活动有多少可以稳妥有效地委托给那些资质稍浅的员工来进行？

    Many employers find it hard to delegate.

    许多雇主觉得很难下放权力。

  • 3.VERB授权，委派（做）If you are delegated to do something, you are given the duty of acting on someone else's behalf by making decisions, voting, or doing some particular work.语法信息
